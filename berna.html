About Me

Hi, my name is Bernnadeth Jane M. Estacio. I am currently a Grade 12 ABM (Accountancy, Business, and Management) student at Culiat High School. I am 17 years old and currently living in Barangay Culiat. I love reading fiction stories on Wattpad, writing my own stories, and watching K-dramas. I also enjoy serving as a reader and being part of the youth leadership at our church. I am the oldest daughter among my siblings, so they always have high expectations of me. In the future, I want to be either a CPA (Certified Public Accountant) or a Math/Science teacher.

Accomplishments

Chosen as a Club Officer

One of my main achievements in IamtheCode is being picked as one of the officers. I am not happy because of the position itself — I am happy because our adviser trusts me in a specific role, even though I know I am not knowledgeable in computers. I accepted it because I know I will learn everything, and they will help me achieve it.

Learning and Socializing Together

During our second meeting, I learned a lot and socialized with others. For me, this was the biggest achievement because as a first-timer in coding, I didn't know everything. I didn't know how to use and manipulate everything in coding, but with the help of my co-coders, I learned, and the next day I did coding on my own.

Successful First Meeting

We had a successful first meeting in IamtheCode. At first I was afraid to make a mistake and make the wrong move in my module. Sometimes I was always asking my fellow students, and I am thankful because they helped me. This was a very important achievement because it was the first time working together as a group, and it taught me how much better things go when we collaborate.

Experience

Managing Responsibilities

My journey in IamtheCode wasn't as easy as I expected, because as a graduating student I need to manage a lot of responsibilities. But when I started doing my modules, I realized I am thankful — I expected nothing, but here I am, a woman with coding skills. Yes, I am not as knowledgeable as others, but I am proud of myself because I learned to do it and I handled all the responsibilities.

Discovering the World of Coding

At first, I wasn't enjoying my IamtheCode journey, because for me it seemed unnecessary since it isn't aligned to my strand (ABM), and it's far from my course in college. But in the end, I realized this is very helpful — not just because I gained knowledge in coding, but because I had the opportunity to meet this world. I am in a generation where we are always using technology, and this experience opened my eyes to that.

Grateful for the Journey

I am truly thankful for IamtheCode because they gave us an opportunity to meet the computer world, to experience how memorable it is, and to learn how to enjoy it while learning. This journey taught me that stepping outside of my comfort zone — even into something that felt far from my path — can open doors I never expected. It was a journey of growth, discovery, and gratitude.
