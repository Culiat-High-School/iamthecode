About Me

I'm Caryl Andrea C. Alo! I'm 17 years old and I live in Barangay Culiat, Quezon City. I am currently taking the Humanities and Social Sciences strand at Culiat High School. I love binge-watching K-Dramas and Movies, and I make sure my schedule is always perfectly organized. I want to become an architect someday and build amazing spaces!

Accomplishments

Learn to Code in HTML

My first achievement was "Learn to Code in HTML" because this was the first coding I ever did. At first, it was difficult — there were so many symbols you had to put in for your work to be correct. But as time went by, my skills improved and I was able to do it properly.

Reaching Rank 5

Before, I only wished that I could make it to rank 10, but while doing the tasks and watching the lesson videos, I was shocked to find myself at rank 5 already. This was a huge achievement for me because from the start, I knew deep down that I might not be able to handle it since the tasks were mostly coding-focused — but I managed to do it properly.

Seeing My Progress

Seeing my achievements and progress was surprising. Back in June and July I was very lazy about doing the tasks during summer break. But when November came, I had already completed so much more. I didn't even notice that I had earned 32 badges, 42 certificates, and 83 completions. My goal became to finish everything while learning from every task and video lesson along the way.

Experience

Club Meeting and Pre-Test

When we gathered for a meeting to check our progress, I still had no progress during those days. So I joined in answering the pre-test for module 3, and I got so many wrong answers. Our president said it would be better to take the pre-test before proceeding to the module. They shared a lot of tips, and I applied all of them. I remembered the answers we discussed during our meeting, so I was able to correct my answers and I passed the test.

Getting Help with My Account

Ma'am Terry was helping me open my account on other devices, because my account couldn't open on anything except my cellphone. I couldn't do some coding tasks that required a laptop or computer, and I was very sad because some of the members were already doing their tasks and I felt like I was being left behind. But in the end, I found a device where my account opened, and I was able to do some coding parts and video lessons.

Overcoming Device Challenges

This experience taught me an important lesson about not giving up when things don't go as planned. Even though I faced difficulties with accessing my account on different devices, the support I received from Ma'am Terry and the other members gave me the motivation to keep going. Once the issue was resolved, I was able to catch up and continue learning and completing my coding tasks and video lessons.
